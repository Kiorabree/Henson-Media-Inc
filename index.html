Welcome to Henson Media

Henson Media was formed in 1990 by Ed Henson and has its primary corporate office in Louisville, KY.

Ed Henson draws on his extensive background of working in the broadcasting business for over thirty-eight years. He understands first hand the challenges faced by owners and operators, and appreciates the importance of confidentiality throughout the process of buying and selling.

Whether, you’re looking to sell after owning and operating a station for many years, want to add or subtract from your group of stations, or are looking to get into ownership, Henson Media can help.

Media Brokerage, Valuations & Ownership

Henson Media, Inc. is a multi-faceted radio company specializing in media brokerage and valuations. We understand that this is a period of rapid and consistent change and strive to help our clients adapt, while adapting to these changes in our own operations.

Brockerage

Looking to sell a station?

Ed Henson would be glad to meet with you, give advice as to the timing of the sale, discuss important terms and conditions that could affect your decision, explore ways to increase your station’s value, and advise you as to pricing.

Once you've decided to list your station with Henson, an extensive presentation is prepared and prospective buyers are then identified and contacted. You will be kept updated throughout the process.

Looking to buy a station?

Ed Henson would be glad to discuss with you market conditions, financing opportunities, trends in the business and possible acquisition targets.

After a representation agreement is signed, prospective sellers will be contacted for you and help negotiate the terms and conditions of the purchase agreement.

Ed is in a position to advise you on pricing and structure of any deal you might decide to pursue. If desired, he would accompany you on station visits and assist in the due diligence process.

Valuations

Henson Media uses several different approaches starting with a description and background of the station. A financial analysis is undertaken, looking at the station’s sales and profit levels and appropriate multiples are applied, as well as a rate of return analysis. After that, there’s usually a review of comparable sales and an analysis of the stations’s assets. Adjustments are made based on the economic vitality of the station’s market, the level of media competition and other factors.
